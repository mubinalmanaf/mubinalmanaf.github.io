01 About

The seam between commercial and data

I manage cost and commercial delivery on large, multi-site residential programmes — and I write the Python, SQL and Power BI that turns raw programme and cost data into audit-ready reporting.

Most people in the built environment do one or the other. I do both, which means the forecast a board sees and the pipeline that produced it are owned by the same person. On a Blackstone-backed residential platform I support interim valuations, cashflow forecasting and portfolio reporting across roughly £1.2bn of GDV, while building the processes that keeps it accurate at scale.

My background is civil engineering — an MSc from Imperial College London and a First Class BEng from Warwick — and I started out designing structural and temporary works on constrained Central London sites. I had to be careful with every calculation when I was taking down a 41m screen house or a 120m flare stack, and I bring the same care to the numbers that underpin a £1.2bn portfolio.

  • C
